Do's and Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Use primary emerald as the only interactive color — one green thread through every screen.
  • Write UI copy in the brand voice everywhere, including errors and empty states.
  • Keep manager-facing surfaces factual and neutral: events, counts, timestamps.
  • Tint every shadow with Evergreen and every background with warmth.
  • Render the cricket in its original linework with clear space around it.
  • Hold WCAG AA on every text pairing; darken derived tokens, not the locked brands.

✗ Don't

  • Don't use red for idleness or anything about a person — error is for disputes and failures only.
  • Don't add scores, gauges, or red/yellow/green ratings of workers anywhere.
  • Don't introduce blue as an accent or focus color.
  • Don't use 0px corners, thin weights, serifs, or body text below 13px.
  • Don't dress the cricket in a top hat or umbrella — no Disney trade dress.
  • Don't crowd: if a screen needs a divider, it more likely needs space.

# Jiminee Pilot — One-Page Brief for the Department

**What it is.** Jiminee is a shared task board for the department's student assistants. Faculty post a task in one sentence; the app's AI expands it into step-by-step instructions a first-week student can follow, with a built-in chat for questions. If a claimed task goes quiet, the student gets a friendly reminder on their laptop or phone. Tasks either get done, or we find out exactly why.

**What we're testing (4–6 weeks, fall semester).** Two numbers, decided in advance:
1. Do faculty delegate more than before? (measured against a short baseline survey)
2. Do ≥80% of assigned tasks get finished without anyone having to personally chase them?

**What it records — and what it never touches.** Jiminee logs task activity only: when a card is claimed, which checklist steps are completed, when it's finished, and whether reminders were answered. It does **not** capture screens, camera, location, keystrokes, or anything else on anyone's device. Every student sees this exact disclosure at signup and acknowledges it before using the board — the acknowledgment is kept on record. The activity record cuts both ways: it's also the student's proof of the work they did.

**The "not my job" fix.** If a student believes a task is out of scope, they flag it with a reason instead of ignoring it. The flag goes to the staff supervisor, who rules — reassign, uphold, or dismiss — with a written note. Every flag and ruling is logged. No more silent standoffs, and performance conversations get facts instead of recollections.

**What we need from the department:**
- **Staff supervisor** — acts as the administrator: ~20 minutes to fill in the "workplace brief" (where supplies live, who to call), rules on flagged tasks as they arise, and runs the 10-minute student onboarding sessions.
- **Department chair** — sign-off on running the pilot with this disclosure language.
- **Participating faculty** — answer one baseline question ("how many tasks a week did you hand to the assistants before this?") and then just… use it. Posting a task takes one sentence.

**Cost and commitment.** Free for the department. No contracts, no installs on department machines (it runs in the browser), and quitting requires nothing but stopping.

**Who's running it.** Mike Giebelhausen (Marketing) built Jiminee and administers the pilot. Questions, concerns, or "please turn it off" requests come straight to him.
